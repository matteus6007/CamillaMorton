Manolo Blahník's and the Tale of the Elves and the Shoemaker

Illustrated by Manolo Blahník

The wondrous rise of famed couture cobbler, Manolo Blahník is retold in this artful memoir that blends the classic tale of The Elves and the Shoemaker with the very charming real life story of how Manolo discovered this exquisite, yet humble, craft. Follow the esteemed designer's journey – and illustrations - from the banana plantations of his childhood to the world of high fashion in a stunning “Happy Ever After” tale, full of mystery, magic, mayhem, and good cheer. Written by international bestselling author Camilla Morton, the book is illustrated exclusively by Manolo Blahník himself.

Manolo Blahník's and the Tale of the Elves and the Shoemaker is the must have fairytale for any girl who loves shoes this holiday season.

About the Fashion Fairytale Memoir series

Each book in this inspired series by fashion writer Camilla Morton inventively reimagines one of our favorite "Once Upon a Time" stories, blending it with the real-life story of a famed fashion designer. Lushly illustrated by the designers themselves these tales illuminate the creative magic in iconic individual's life and career. The result is an intriguing combination of whimsy and memoir.

In this contemporary twist on the tale of the Elves and the Shoemaker, Manolo Blahník's delightful drawings evoke the past, envision the future and, of course, evidence the indelible footprint he's made on fashion history. His illustrations take us from the banana plantations of his childhood to his current reign at the top of high fashion footwear design, as well as playfully hint at all the adventures he's had and friends he's met along the way.

About the designer

Manol Blahník, whose career spans more than 30 years, is one of the world's most celebrated and imaginative footwear designers. His shoes have enchanted an international set of adoring and loyal devotees around the globe. Born in the Canary Islands and later educated in Geneva, he moved in 1965 to Paris where he intended a career in theatre design. On a visit to New York in 1970 he met with the legendary editor-in-chief of American Vogue, Diana Vreeland who told him, ‘Do shoes'. He has been doing so expertly ever since-- on the runway, the red carpet and in every girl's wildest dreams.
