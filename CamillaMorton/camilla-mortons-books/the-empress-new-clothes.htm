In DIANE VON FURSTENBERG AND THE TALE OF THE EMPRESS'S NEW CLOTHES, the third book in the unique Fashion Fairy Tale Memoir series, royalty and fashion collide in a delightful new 'happily ever after' to inspire women how to feel empowered and confident in their own skin.

Featuring iconic DVF prints, bold colour with uplifting attitude fashion writer and internationally bestselling author, Camilla Morton celebrates the story of fashion designer Diane von Furstenberg and weaves a cautionary tale for the Empress in all of us. Discover the story of a modern day Empress and her dangerous fashion obsession - and find out how New York's iconic fashion designer, Warhol muse, Entrepreneur and real-life princess –Diane von Furstenberg - comes to the rescue.

Here is a tale of two heroines in a tale that challenges style and protocol to create a wardrobe for women by a working-woman to help them find themselves and feel beautiful inside and out.

Be enchanted as fairy tale and bold fashions mix magically in this favorite tale re-imagined by Morton and with exclusive illustrations by the DVF Studio

About the Fashion Fairytale Memoir series

Each book in this inspired series from fashion writer Camilla Morton inventively re-imagines one of our favorite "Once Upon a Time" stories blending with it the real-life story of a famed fashion designer. Featuring lush illustrations drawn and collaged by the designers themselves, these tales illuminate each iconic individual's creative magic while celebrating their unique lives and extraordinary careers. The result is an intriguing combination of whimsy and memoir.

About the designer

Diane von Furstenberg arrived in New York in 1970 with a suitcase full of her iconic wrap dresses and changed the way women dressed. DVF has pioneered fashion and business, built and sold a cosmetics company and founded a publishing house, and she supports many philanthropic causes— especially for women and the environment. DVF returned from a hiatus to fashion in 1997, relaunching the signature wrap to international acclaim. In 2005 the Council of Fashion Designers of America (CFDA) awarded her a Lifetime Achievement Award, and the following year named her as the Council President.
